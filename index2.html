Why driving after 55 is not the same:

What REALLY happens after 55?
  • Your eyesight is reduced drastically.
  • Your hearing is no longer as it used to be.
  • After 37+ years, you tend to forget defensive driving techniques.
  • Cars are continuously evolving and come with new safety features, while your skills are left behind.
  • And worst of all, the communication between your senses and brain cells is slower... much slower.

For example, a younger me would slam the brakes instantly if the car in front of me would stop unexpectedly, but I’m not so sure about the 62-year-old version of me.

Although it hurts my ego, my feelings, and every cell in my body, I have to admit it: the older you get, the slower your reflexes get, and it’s less safe to drive.

Now, this isn’t a story about replacing driving with walking… I would never do that unless it’s absolutely necessary. That being said…

Driving after a certain age is also one of those things that’s hard to talk about with your children. They wanted me to stop driving for 2 years, but I didn’t. I didn’t want to give up my independence and freedom, but I didn’t want to make roads less safe than they already are...

I knew I had to do something to sharpen my driving skills before anything would happen!
